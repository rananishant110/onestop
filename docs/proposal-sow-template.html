1. Project Overview

This proposal outlines the scope, approach, estimated timeline, and commercial terms for designing and developing a wholesale ordering platform for the client. The objective is to create a digital solution that allows wholesale customers to browse products, view pricing, place orders, and manage repeat purchases more efficiently.

Depending on the final approved scope, the solution may include a customer-facing web application, optional mobile applications, and an internal admin dashboard for managing products, pricing, orders, customers, and operations.

Recommended approach: begin with an MVP focused on the most critical ordering workflows, then expand into mobile apps, deeper integrations, and advanced automation in later phases.

2. Project Objectives

  • Simplify the wholesale ordering process for customers
  • Reduce manual order handling via phone, WhatsApp, and email
  • Improve ordering accuracy and reduce operational overhead
  • Support repeat purchasing and account-based wholesale ordering
  • Provide internal visibility into orders, customers, and product management
  • Create a scalable digital platform for future growth

3. Proposed Solution

Based on discovery and scope confirmation, the proposed platform may include:

  • Customer Ordering Portal (Web)
  • Mobile Application (optional / later phase)
  • Admin Dashboard
  • Order Management System
  • Customer-specific Pricing
  • Delivery / Pickup Scheduling
  • Invoice / Payment Visibility
  • Third-party Integrations such as accounting, ERP, inventory, or payment systems

4. Scope of Work

Phase 1 — Discovery and Planning

Includes requirements gathering, process analysis, feature prioritization, and solution planning.

  • Stakeholder meetings
  • Requirements gathering
  • Business workflow mapping
  • MVP definition
  • Technical recommendations
  • Phased roadmap

Deliverables: Requirements summary, feature list, user flows, MVP definition, and implementation roadmap.

Phase 2 — UI/UX Design

Includes design of the customer and admin experience.

  • Wireframes
  • Responsive layout planning
  • High-fidelity UI screens
  • Review and feedback rounds

Deliverables: Approved wireframes, UI designs, and optional clickable prototype.

Phase 3 — Development

The development phase may include the following modules depending on scope:

  • User authentication and account management
  • Customer-specific product catalog
  • Search, filters, and categories
  • Cart and checkout flow
  • Reorder from previous orders
  • Order history and status tracking
  • Delivery / pickup scheduling
  • Admin management for customers, products, pricing, and orders
  • Reports and operational dashboards
  • Payment gateway integration if applicable
  • Accounting / inventory / ERP integrations if applicable

Phase 4 — Testing and Launch

  • QA testing
  • Bug fixing
  • User acceptance testing
  • Deployment and launch support

Deliverables: Production-ready application deployment and launch support.

5. Example MVP Scope

A recommended MVP for a wholesale ordering platform may include:

  • Customer login
  • Product catalog browsing
  • Customer-specific pricing
  • Cart and order placement
  • Reorder previous orders
  • Order history
  • Admin dashboard
  • Product management
  • Customer management
  • Order management

Optional features such as native mobile apps, advanced route delivery logic, barcode scanning, and ERP sync can be planned as future phases.

6. Out of Scope

Unless specifically included in the final scope, the following items are considered out of scope:

  • Custom ERP development
  • Advanced warehouse management features
  • Marketplace or multi-vendor functionality
  • Route optimization engines
  • Complex loyalty or referral systems
  • Multilingual implementation
  • Additional integrations not listed in the approved scope
  • Post-launch features beyond agreed support/warranty terms

7. Assumptions
